Prealgebra · Lesson 9.1

Percents as Fractions

Solve this lesson — free →All lessons

Percent means per hundred, from the Latin per centum. So \(73\%\) is literally \(\frac{73}{100}\), a fraction with a hidden denominator of \(100\). The little \(\%\) sign is just standing in for that buried \(/100\).

Key idea
Percent means per hundred. The word comes from the Latin per centum, so \(x\%\) is just a shorter name for the fraction \(\frac{x}{100}\), with the \(\%\) sign standing in for the hidden denominator. Counting a portion out of exactly \(100\) hands you the percent straight away, and any other percent is the same idea with \(100\) sitting quietly underneath. A percent is nothing new, it is a fraction you already know how to handle.
Your phone storage is \(80\%\) full. Write \(80\%\) as a fraction in lowest terms.
Show solution
Start by reading the percent literally. \(80\%\) means 80 per hundred, so write it over 100. $$80\% = \frac{80}{100}$$ Now reduce, just like any fraction. The greatest common factor of 80 and 100 is 20, so divide both the top and the bottom by 20. $$\frac{80}{100} = \frac{80 \div 20}{100 \div 20} = \frac{4}{5}$$ A quick check, \(\tfrac{4}{5}\) of the storage is the same slice as \(80\%\) of it, just written in lowest terms. So the answer is \(\boxed{4/5}\).
Key idea
Percent to fraction. Write \(r\%\) as \(\frac{r}{100}\), since percent only ever meant per hundred, then divide the top and bottom by their greatest common factor until nothing else divides both. The hidden denominator was \(100\) the whole time. You just drag it into the open and reduce to lowest terms, exactly like any other fraction.
A bakery sold \(250\%\) as many sourdough loaves as last Saturday. Percents can exceed \(100\). Write \(250\%\) as a fraction in lowest terms.
Show solution
A percent is a number of hundredths, so I put the percent over \(100\). $$250\% = \frac{250}{100}$$ Going past \(100\) is fine, it just means the fraction is bigger than \(1\). Now I reduce. The greatest common factor of \(250\) and \(100\) is \(50\), so I divide top and bottom by \(50\). $$\frac{250}{100} = \frac{250 \div 50}{100 \div 50} = \frac{5}{2}$$ That matches the "two and a half times" idea, since \(\tfrac{5}{2}\) is \(2.5\). So \(250\%\) in lowest terms is \(\boxed{5/2}\).
A glacier retreated by \(-45\%\) over a decade. A percent can be negative. Write \(-45\%\) as a fraction in lowest terms.
Show solution
The percent sign means "per hundred," so put the number over \(100\) and let the minus sign stay with it. That gives \(-45\% = \tfrac{-45}{100}\). Now simplify. Both \(45\) and \(100\) are divisible by \(5\), so divide the top and bottom by \(5\). The top becomes \(-45 \div 5 = -9\) and the bottom becomes \(100 \div 5 = 20\), so \(\tfrac{-45}{100} = \tfrac{-9}{20}\). Since \(9\) and \(20\) share no common factor bigger than \(1\), this is lowest terms. \(\boxed{-9/20}\)
Key idea
No ceiling on percents. A percent is never trapped between \(0\) and \(100\), because \(x\%\) is only ever \(\frac{x}{100}\) and the numerator is free to be anything. More than one whole shows up as more than \(100\%\), so \(250\% = \frac{250}{100} = \frac{5}{2}\), while \(100\% = \frac{100}{100} = 1\) is exactly one whole. The top can dip below \(1\) or even go negative. The \(100\) was never a wall, it was just the denominator underneath.
A coupon takes \(38\%\) off. Rewrite \(38\%\) as a decimal.
Show solution
Start from the meaning of a percent. \(38\%=\dfrac{38}{100}=38\div 100\). Dividing by \(100\) moves the decimal point two places to the left. Begin at \(38.\), hop the point left once to get \(3.8\), then left again to land at \(0.38\), writing a zero in the now empty tenths-and-ones gap. So \(38\%=\boxed{0.38}\).
A bridge sensor drifts by \(0.4\%\). Write \(0.4\%\) as a decimal.
Show solution
A percent is a count out of one hundred, so \(0.4\%\) means \(\tfrac{0.4}{100}\). Dividing by \(100\) slides the decimal point two places to the left. Starting at \(0.4\), one slide gives \(0.04\) and a second slide gives \(0.004\), with zeros filling the empty places. So \(0.4\% = \boxed{0.004}\).
Key idea
Percent \(\leftrightarrow\) decimal. To turn a percent into a decimal, divide by \(100\), which slides the point two places to the left, padding with zeros when you run out of digits. To go back, multiply by \(100\), sliding the point two places right. Same shift, opposite directions, so the two moves undo each other. If you forget which way, lean on the meaning, since \(38\%\) is \(38\) out of \(100\), which is \(0.38\).
Your fitness app shows daily goal progress as the decimal \(0.41\). Write \(0.41\) as a percent. Give just the number.
Show solution
A decimal becomes a percent when you multiply it by \(100\), and multiplying by \(100\) just slides the decimal point two places to the right. Start with \(0.41\). Slide the point past the \(4\) to get \(4.1\), then past the \(1\) to get \(41\). So \(0.41 \times 100 = 41\), which means \(0.41 = 41\%\). The answer is \(\boxed{41}\).
A race lap timer reads \(0.073\) of the way through the final lap. Write \(0.073\) as a percent. Give just the number.
Show solution
A percent means how many hundredths, so to convert a decimal to a percent you multiply by \(100\). Multiplying by \(100\) slides the decimal point two places to the right. Starting from \(0.073\), one slide gives \(0.73\) and a second slide gives \(7.3\). Notice there were three digits after the point, so only two of them clear the point and the last one stays behind in the tenths place. That is why the percent is itself a decimal. So \(0.073 = 7.3\%\), and the number you type is \(\boxed{7.3}\).
Fun fact
The \(\%\) sign is a frozen abbreviation: Italian merchants wrote per c., which blurred across centuries into the slashed pair of zeros used today. The cousin sign ‰ means per thousand.
A garden bed is \(\tfrac{3}{4}\) planted. A percent has a hidden denominator of \(100\). Write \(\tfrac{3}{4}\) as a percent. Give just the number.
Show solution
Rewrite \(\tfrac{3}{4}\) as an equal fraction whose denominator is \(100\). Since \(4 \times 25 = 100\), multiply the top and bottom by the same \(25\). $$\frac{3}{4} = \frac{3 \times 25}{4 \times 25} = \frac{75}{100}.$$ A fraction over \(100\) is read directly as a percent, so \(\tfrac{75}{100} = 75\%\). The percent is \(\boxed{75}\).
Key idea
Fraction to percent. Rescale the fraction until the denominator is \(100\), and the numerator is then the percent, because percent already means out of one hundred. This lands cleanly when the denominator divides \(100\), as with \(2, 4, 5, 10, 20, 25, 50,\) and \(100\), so \(\frac{4}{5} = \frac{80}{100} = 80\%\). When the denominator does not divide \(100\) neatly, multiply the whole fraction by \(100\) instead, the same measuring-against-a-hundred that works for any fraction at all.
A bakery scales a recipe to \(\tfrac{8}{5}\) of the original. Since \(\tfrac{8}{5} > 1\), the percent lands above \(100\). Write \(\tfrac{8}{5}\) as a percent. Answer with just the number.
Show solution
A percent is a count out of \(100\), so rewrite \(\tfrac{8}{5}\) with a denominator of \(100\). Since \(5\times 20=100\), multiply the top and bottom by \(20\). $$\frac{8}{5}=\frac{8\times 20}{5\times 20}=\frac{160}{100}.$$ A fraction over \(100\) reads straight off as a percent, so \(\tfrac{160}{100}=160\%\). You can check this by multiplying instead, \(\tfrac{8}{5}\times 100=\tfrac{800}{5}=160\). Because \(\tfrac{8}{5}\) is bigger than one whole, the percent climbed past \(100\), exactly as expected. \(\boxed{160}\)
A recipe calls for \(\tfrac{9}{8}\) cups of oats. Since eighths do not divide \(100\) evenly, multiply by \(100\) and simplify. Write \(\tfrac{9}{8}\) as a percent. Give just the number.
Show solution
A fraction becomes a percent when you multiply it by \(100\), so start with \(\tfrac{9}{8}\times 100\). Writing \(100\) over the eight gives $$\frac{9}{8}\times 100=\frac{9\times 100}{8}=\frac{900}{8}.$$ Divide the top and bottom by \(4\) to get \(\tfrac{225}{2}\), and dividing \(225\) by \(2\) gives \(112.5\). The result is more than \(100\) because \(\tfrac{9}{8}\) is more than one whole, and it carries a \(.5\) because halving an odd number leaves a half. So as a percent it is \(\boxed{112.5}\), meaning \(112.5\%\) of a single scoop.
Key idea
Three costumes, one number. A percent, a fraction, and a decimal are three names for the same value, so you can move between them whenever you like, sliding the point two places to swap a decimal and a percent, or going over \(100\) to swap a percent and a fraction. The real payoff is comparing. When two values arrive in different forms your eyes cannot judge which is bigger, so put them both in the same form first, usually all decimals or all over \(100\), and then the larger one is obvious.
Room A chose pizza at \(65\%\). Room B chose pizza at \(\tfrac{3}{5}\). Convert both to decimals and pick the larger share. Type \(1\) for Room A or \(2\) for Room B.
Show solution
Put both shares in the same form, decimals. Room A is \(65\%\). To turn a percent into a decimal, divide by \(100\), which slides the point two places left, so \(65\% = 0.65\). Room B is \(\tfrac{3}{5}\). Dividing \(3\) by \(5\) gives \(0.60\), and you can check it by rewriting \(\tfrac{3}{5} = \tfrac{6}{10} = 0.60\). Now both are decimals, so compare. Since \(0.65 > 0.60\), Room A has the larger pizza share, which is option 1. \(\boxed{1}\)
A sales tax is \(12.5\%\). Write \(12.5\%\) as a fraction in lowest terms.
Show solution
Percent means per hundred, so \(12.5\%\) becomes \(\tfrac{12.5}{100}\). A tidy fraction has whole numbers top and bottom, so multiply both by \(10\) to slide the decimal away. That gives $$\frac{12.5}{100} = \frac{12.5 \times 10}{100 \times 10} = \frac{125}{1000}.$$ Now reduce. Both \(125\) and \(1{,}000\) share the factor \(125\), and dividing each by it gives \(\tfrac{1}{8}\). So twelve and a half percent is exactly one eighth, which makes sense since \(\tfrac{1}{8}\) of a hundred is \(12.5\). \(\boxed{1/8}\)

Review problems

Review 1. A survey found \(55\%\) of students bike to school. Write \(55\%\) as a fraction in lowest terms.
Show solution
Percent means "per hundred," so \(55\%\) is \(55\) out of \(100\). $$55\% = \frac{55}{100}$$ Now reduce. The numerator and denominator both share a factor of \(5\), so divide each by \(5\). $$\frac{55}{100} = \frac{55 \div 5}{100 \div 5} = \frac{11}{20}$$ Since \(11\) is prime and does not divide \(20\), the fraction is in lowest terms. \(\boxed{11/20}\)
Review 2. A pollster reports \(24\%\) of people surveyed had never tasted dark chocolate. Write \(24\%\) as a fraction in lowest terms.
Show solution
The word percent means per hundred, so \(24\%\) is \(24\) out of \(100\). $$24\% = \frac{24}{100}$$ Now simplify. The greatest factor shared by \(24\) and \(100\) is \(4\), so divide both the numerator and the denominator by \(4\). $$\frac{24}{100} = \frac{24 \div 4}{100 \div 4} = \frac{6}{25}$$ Since \(6\) and \(25\) share no common factor larger than \(1\), this is fully reduced. \(\boxed{6/25}\)
Review 3. A loyalty card stamps your total at \(320\%\) of last month. Write \(320\%\) as a fraction in lowest terms.
Show solution
Percent means per hundred, so \(320\%=\tfrac{320}{100}\). The greatest common factor of \(320\) and \(100\) is \(20\), so divide both parts by it. That gives \(\tfrac{320\div 20}{100\div 20}=\tfrac{16}{5}\). Since \(16\) and \(5\) share no factor bigger than \(1\), this is lowest terms, and it is more than one whole because \(16>5\). \(\boxed{16/5}\)
Review 4. A deep freezer drifted \(-8\%\) from its set point. Write \(-8\%\) as a fraction in lowest terms.
Show solution
Percent means per hundred, so the number sits over \(100\) and keeps its sign. That gives \(-8\% = \tfrac{-8}{100}\). The top and bottom share a common factor of \(4\), so divide both by \(4\). The numerator becomes \(-8 \div 4 = -2\) and the denominator becomes \(100 \div 4 = 25\). Since \(2\) and \(25\) have no common factor left, the fraction is in lowest terms. So \(-8\%\) equals \(\boxed{-2/25}\).
Review 5. A weather app says the chance of rain is \(73\%\). Write \(73\%\) as a decimal.
Show solution
A percent is just a number of hundredths, so \(73\%\) means \(\tfrac{73}{100}\). Dividing by \(100\) slides the decimal point two places to the left, turning \(73.\) into \(0.73\). So the decimal form is \(\boxed{0.73}\).
Review 6. Write \(108\%\) as a decimal.
Show solution
A percent is a number out of \(100\), so to write it as a decimal you divide by \(100\), which slides the point two places to the left. Begin with \(108\), really \(108.0\), and move the point two places left to land between the \(1\) and the first \(0\). That gives \(108\% = \tfrac{108}{100} = 1.08\). Notice the result is more than \(1\), which makes sense because \(108\%\) is more than a whole. So the decimal is \(\boxed{1.08}\).
Review 7. A lab label reads \(0.6\%\) saline. Write \(0.6\%\) as a decimal.
Show solution
A percent is just a number over 100, so \(0.6\%\) means \(\tfrac{0.6}{100}\). Dividing by 100 slides the decimal point two places to the left. Starting from \(0.6\), one place left gives \(0.06\), and a second place gives \(0.006\), with placeholder zeros filling the gaps. So \(0.6\%\) as a decimal is \(\boxed{0.006}\).
Review 8. A probe is filled to \(0.47\) of its tank. Write \(0.47\) as a percent. Give just the number.
Show solution
To turn a decimal into a percent, multiply by \(100\), which slides the point two places to the right. So \(0.47 \times 100 = 47\), giving \(47\%\). You can also picture it as \(0.47 = \tfrac{47}{100}\), and a denominator of \(100\) reads straight off as \(47\) per hundred. The number to post is \(\boxed{47}\).
Review 9. A grain of gold dust registers \(0.025\) of a carat. Write \(0.025\) as a percent. Give just the number.
Show solution
To go from a decimal to a percent, multiply by \(100\), which slides the decimal point two places to the right. $$0.025 \times 100 = 2.5$$ Sliding the point from \(0.025\) two places to the right lands between the \(2\) and the \(5\), so the value is \(2.5\%\). The number alone is \(\boxed{2.5}\).
Review 10. A trail map marks \(\tfrac{7}{20}\) of its loops as beginner-friendly. Write \(\tfrac{7}{20}\) as a percent. Give just the number.
Show solution
A percent is just a fraction with a denominator of \(100\), so the plan is to rewrite \(\tfrac{7}{20}\) with \(100\) on the bottom. Multiplying \(20\) by \(5\) gives \(100\), so multiply the top by \(5\) as well to keep the same value. $$\frac{7}{20} = \frac{7 \times 5}{20 \times 5} = \frac{35}{100}$$ A numerator of \(35\) out of \(100\) means \(35\%\), so the percent number is \(\boxed{35}\).
Review 11. A juice bottle is \(\tfrac{5}{8}\) real fruit. Write \(\tfrac{5}{8}\) as a percent. Give just the number.
Show solution
To turn a fraction into a percent, multiply it by \(100\). $$\frac{5}{8}\times 100=\frac{500}{8}=62.5$$ So \(\tfrac{5}{8}\) of the bottle is \(62.5\%\) fruit, and the number is \(\boxed{62.5}\).
Review 12. Write \(\tfrac{11}{5}\) as a percent. Give just the number.
Show solution
A percent is just a count of parts per hundred, so to write a fraction as a percent you multiply it by \(100\). That gives \(\tfrac{11}{5}\times 100=\tfrac{1{,}100}{5}\). Dividing, \(1{,}100\div 5=220\), so \(\tfrac{11}{5}=220\%\). That makes sense, since \(\tfrac{11}{5}\) is more than \(2\), and \(220\%\) is more than \(200\%\). The number is \(\boxed{220}\).
Review 13. A pool is \(87.5\%\) full. Write \(87.5\%\) as a fraction in lowest terms.
Show solution
Percent means per hundred, so \(87.5\%=\tfrac{87.5}{100}\). Multiply the top and bottom by \(10\) to clear the decimal, which gives \(\tfrac{875}{1000}\). Now reduce. Both numbers are divisible by \(125\), and \(875\div125=7\) while \(1{,}000\div125=8\), so the fraction becomes \(\tfrac{7}{8}\). Since \(7\) is prime and does not divide \(8\), this is lowest terms. \(\boxed{7/8}\)
Review 14. Option 1 is \(\tfrac{9}{20}\) and option 2 is \(44\%\). Which value is larger? Type \(1\) or \(2\).
Show solution
Put both into percent form so they can be compared head to head. For the fraction, scale \(\tfrac{9}{20}\) to a denominator of \(100\) by multiplying top and bottom by \(5\), which gives \(\tfrac{45}{100}=45\%\). Now line them up, \(45\%\) against \(44\%\). Since \(45\%>44\%\), option 1 is the larger value. \(\boxed{1}\)
