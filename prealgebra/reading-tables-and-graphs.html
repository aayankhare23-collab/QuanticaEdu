Reading Tables and Graphs

Solve this lesson — free →All lessons

In 10.6 you squeezed a whole list down to one number with the mean, median, and mode. Now we go the other way and read data that someone has already arranged for you. A good display shows at a glance what a bare list hides, the biggest category, a trend over time, how a whole splits into parts. In this lesson you will read tables, bar and line graphs, pie charts, stem-and-leaf plots, and histograms.

The table above shows how many boxes of cookies three scout troops sold in October and November. Birch's Total is highlighted with a ? because it is missing. Complete the table by finding Birch's Total. Give the number.
