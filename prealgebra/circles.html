Circles

Solve this lesson — free →All lessons

Every shape so far has had straight sides you could count and add. A circle has none. Yet it still has a length running all the way around it and a flat space filling its inside, a perimeter and an area, just like the others. Measuring them turns up one famous number, \(\pi\), that hides inside every circle ever drawn. Once you have hold of it, you can find the distance around and the space within for any circle at all.

Key idea
Radius and diameter. Every point of a circle sits the same distance from the center, and that distance is the radius. A straight line all the way across through the center is the diameter, and since it is just two radii laid end to end, the diameter is twice the radius, \(d = 2r\) (so \(r = \frac{d}{2}\)).
