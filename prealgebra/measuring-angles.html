Prealgebra · Lesson 11.1

Measuring Angles

Solve this lesson, free →All lessons

Geometry begins with the plainest things you can imagine. A single point. Two points joined make a segment, stretch it one way and you get a ray, stretch it both ways and you get a line. Now let two rays start from the same point. They open up, and the space between them is an angle. In this lesson you will learn to name angles and to measure how far they open, counted in degrees.

Key idea
Angles, names, and degrees. Let two rays start at the same point and the opening between them is an angle. That shared point is the vertex and the two rays are the sides. Name an angle with three letters, always keeping the vertex in the middle, so $\angle ABC$ is the angle at $B$; the order of the outer two letters is free, so $\angle ABC=\angle CBA$, and when only one angle sits at a point you may just write $\angle B$. We measure how far an angle opens in degrees. A full turn all the way around is $360^\circ$, a straight line is half of that at $180^\circ$, and a square corner, a right angle, is $90^\circ$; a protractor reads the degrees straight off. Sort angles by how far they open: acute is below $90^\circ$, right is exactly $90^\circ$, obtuse is between $90^\circ$ and $180^\circ$, straight is exactly $180^\circ$, and reflex is more than $180^\circ$, the big way around the outside.
