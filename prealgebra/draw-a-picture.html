Some problems arrive as a wall of words, and the words are designed to tangle you. The strongest first move is often not a calculation at all. It is a sketch. A path becomes a map, a schedule becomes a timeline, a set of clues becomes dots and arrows, and suddenly the answer is sitting in plain sight. This lesson is about drawing your way past the tangle.

A baker slices a baguette into \(9\) pieces, and each cut takes \(30\) seconds. Sketch the baguette with its cut marks. How many minutes does the slicing take?
